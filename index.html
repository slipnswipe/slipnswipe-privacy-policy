Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 30, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Ethan Lim ("I", "me", or "the developer") handles information in connection with the Slip 'n Swipe mobile application ("the App"). By using the App, you acknowledge this policy.

1. Information I Collect

I do not directly collect, store, or transmit any personal information from users of this App. The App does not require you to create an account or provide any personal details.

The only data stored by the App is saved locally on your device: your high score and user preferences (music on/off, sound on/off, haptics on/off, and whether you have disabled ads). This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to me.

2. Third-Party Services

2a. Google AdMob (Advertising)

The App uses Google AdMob to display advertisements. AdMob is only active if you have not disabled ads in the App's settings. If you disable ads, the AdMob SDK is not initialised and no ad-related data collection occurs through this App.

When AdMob is active, Google may collect the following data from your device:

  • Advertising identifier — your device's Android Advertising ID (Android), used for ad targeting and measurement. On iOS, the IDFA is only accessed if you grant permission via Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt; if you decline, AdMob serves non-personalised ads without accessing your IDFA.
  • IP address — used to estimate your general location for ad relevance and fraud prevention
  • Device identifiers — device-bounded identifiers used for analytics and fraud prevention
  • Device information — device model, operating system version, and screen size used for ad rendering and targeting
  • App information — app name and version used for ad serving context
  • Ad interaction data — advertisements you have seen, taps, and video views, used to improve advertising performance
  • App performance data — app launch time, hang rate, and energy usage for diagnostic purposes
  • Crash logs — non-user-related crash data used to diagnose SDK issues

This data is collected and processed by Google LLC, not by me. AdMob may use this data to serve personalised advertisements based on your interests. Ad targeting involves automated profiling — Google uses automated systems to select which ads to show you based on inferred interests and device signals.

AdMob also shares data with Google's advertising technology partners for ad serving, measurement, and fraud prevention purposes. For a list of these partners, see: https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites

For more information, please review Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Data collected by AdMob may be transferred to and processed in the United States and other countries. Google relies on Standard Contractual Clauses and other appropriate safeguards for international data transfers.

2b. App Tracking Transparency (iOS)

On iOS 14.5 and later, Apple requires apps to request permission before accessing your device's Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA) for tracking purposes. When you first open the App on iOS, you will see a system prompt asking whether you allow the App to track your activity across other apps and websites for advertising purposes. This prompt is powered by Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework.

  • If you allow tracking: AdMob may access your IDFA to serve personalised advertisements.
  • If you decline tracking: your IDFA is not accessed, and AdMob will serve non-personalised advertisements only. This does not reduce the number of ads shown.

You can change your choice at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.

4. Advertising Choices & Opt-Out

You can control personalised advertising through your device settings:

  • iOS: When prompted, decline the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) request to prevent access to your IDFA. You can also go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → disable "Allow Apps to Request to Track". AdMob will serve non-personalised ads if tracking is denied.
  • Android: Go to Settings → Privacy → Ads → select "Delete advertising ID" or enable "Opt out of Ads Personalisation".

You can also manage Google's use of your data for advertising at: https://adssettings.google.com

Alternatively, you can disable ads entirely within the App via the Settings screen, which prevents the AdMob SDK from initialising. Note: disabling ads within the App is a permanent, one-way action — there is no in-app option to re-enable ads once they have been disabled.

Note: opting out of personalised ads does not mean you will see fewer ads — it means the ads you see will not be tailored to your interests.

5. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

The App is a general-audience arcade game that may be used by children of any age. I comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the 2025 amended COPPA Rule. I do not directly collect personal information from any user, including children under 13.

However, Google AdMob — the advertising service used by this App — may collect device identifiers, device information, IP addresses, and usage data from all users, including children under 13, for advertising and analytics purposes as described in Section 2a. I have not designated this App as child-directed within AdMob; if you wish to prevent personalised ad data collection for a child, use your device's advertising settings to opt out (see Section 4) or disable ads in the App's Settings screen.

6. Data Retention

I do not retain any personal data. All app data (high score and preferences) is stored locally on your device and is deleted when you uninstall the App or clear the App's data in your device settings.

Data collected by Google AdMob is retained according to Google's data retention policies. Please refer to Google's Privacy Policy for details.

7. Security

Since all data produced by this App is stored locally on your device and I do not operate any servers or databases, there is no data held by me that could be compromised. Local data is protected by your device's standard app sandboxing. Data transmitted by Google AdMob is encrypted in transit using TLS (Transport Layer Security).

8b. European Economic Area (EEA) / UK Users — GDPR

If you are located in the EEA or UK, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) gives you the following rights regarding personal data processed in connection with this App (primarily by Google AdMob):

  • Right of access — to obtain confirmation of whether data about you is processed and to receive a copy
  • Right to rectification — to correct inaccurate personal data
  • Right to erasure — to request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to restriction — to restrict how your data is processed
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling for advertising
  • Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions — ad targeting involves automated profiling; you may object to this via the opt-out controls in Section 4

For data held by Google, exercise these rights via https://myaccount.google.com.

The legal basis for Google AdMob's processing is consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR) for personalised advertising and profiling, and legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) for fraud prevention and analytics. For personalised advertising, consent is required and may be withdrawn at any time through your device advertising settings (see Section 4).

Google transfers data internationally to the United States and other countries. These transfers are governed by Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission.

If you have concerns about data processing, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local EU/UK Data Protection Authority. A list of EEA supervisory authorities is available at: https://edpb.europa.eu/about-edpb/about-edpb/members_en
